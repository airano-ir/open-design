City Office of Climate & BuildingsPolicy brief 26-14 · Public session

Disclose first, then set performance standards with measured evidence

Mandatory energy disclosure for large buildings creates the data and market transparency required for a fair, enforceable performance policy.

Resolution requested Adopt annual energy disclosure for buildings over 4,500 m² beginning in 2027, with a two-year support period before penalties. Authority: Municipal Buildings Act §18 · Review date: December 2028

Three findings support a phased disclosure mandate

1
Large buildings drive a disproportionate share of operational emissions.

The covered 12% of properties account for an estimated 48% of building energy use.

2
Owners cannot manage performance against an invisible benchmark.

Only 27% currently report normalized energy intensity in a comparable form.

3
Disclosure is lower burden than immediate performance penalties.

A support-first phase reveals data quality and capacity gaps before enforcement.

  • Comparable performance information
